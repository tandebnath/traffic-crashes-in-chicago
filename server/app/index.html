Traffic Crashes in ChicagoKinda fast, really furious
Year 2019
In 2019, there were a total of117,760 traffic-related crashes in the city of Chicago.

As per Illinois statute, only crashes with a property damage value of $1,500 or more or involving bodily injury to any person(s) and that happen on a public roadway and that involve at least one moving vehicle, except bike dooring, are considered reportable crashes. However, the Chicago Police Department records every reported traffic crash event, regardless of the statute of limitations.

Of these, 31,953 were Hit and Run cases.

Well, who's behind the wheels?

An age-wise distribution reveals the majority of crashes occur when an 18-39 year old is driving. Additionally, fun (?) fact: a 90+ year old driver was involved in 229 crashes.

A Comparative Analysis

Types of Injuries and Their Distribution Across the Week

We see how the injuries in the reported crashes are distributed across the different days of the week. We observed that in both years (2019 and 2022), most of the injuries were reported during the weekends (between Friday and Sunday) and lower during the weekdays. In addition, the data also showed that fewer crashes resulted to fatal injuries. The majority of crashes resulted to non-incapacitating, non-evident and a few incapacitating injuries.

Comparing the Primary Causes of Crashes and their Respective Damages

For both years, we wanted to compare the major causes of the reported crashes and evaluate the damage involved in these incidents. The damages were classified into 3 categories, (below 500, 500-1500 and above 1500 dollars). In both years, the majority of the accidents involve damages that are over 1500 dollars. Interestingly, the primary cause of the majory of crashes in both years was not determined. However, other major causes of the accidents included failing to yield, following too closely, failing to reduce speed, improper lane usage, turning and overtaking.
